About Us

Bionexus is a scientific community that aims to enhance students' passion for biology through experiments, research, competitions, and collaborative projects.


We provide a space where young scientists can grow, explore, innovate, and connect with biology experts. Our club is dedicated to fostering the next generation of biologists through hands-on experiences and mentorship.


At Bionexus, we believe that biology is not just a subject but a gateway to understanding life itself. Our members engage in various activities that challenge their thinking and expand their knowledge beyond the classroom.


Founded in 2020, Bionexus has grown to become one of the most active and successful clubs at Maadi STEM School for Girls. Our members have won numerous awards in national and international competitions.


We organize regular field trips to research institutions, host guest lectures from leading biologists, and provide resources for students to conduct their own research projects.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To cultivate a generation of innovative biologists who will lead scientific discovery and address global challenges through biological research and application.

We envision a future where our members become pioneers in biotechnology, medicine, environmental conservation, and scientific education.

Our Mission

To provide an engaging, supportive environment where students can explore biology beyond the classroom through hands-on experiments, research projects, and collaboration with experts.

We aim to foster critical thinking, scientific curiosity, and ethical responsibility in our members while preparing them for success in scientific careers.

Our Branches

Biochemistry & Cell Biology

Biochemistry explores the chemical processes within living organisms, focusing on molecules like proteins, carbohydrates, lipids, and nucleic acids.

70 Sessions
Show More

In this branch, students study metabolic pathways, enzyme kinetics, and cellular signaling. They learn techniques like chromatography, spectrophotometry, and cell culture.

Recent projects include studying the effects of antioxidants on cell aging and analyzing protein structures using computational tools.

Genetics & Evolution

Genetics studies heredity and variation in living organisms, while evolution explores how species change over time through natural selection.

65 Sessions
Show More

Students in this branch work with DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis, and genetic mapping. They also study population genetics and phylogenetic analysis.

Current research includes analyzing genetic diversity in local plant species and studying inheritance patterns in Drosophila.

Neuroscience

Neuroscience examines the structure and function of the nervous system, from molecular and cellular levels to behavior and cognition.

55 Sessions
Show More

This branch covers neuroanatomy, neural signaling, sensory systems, and cognitive processes. Students learn about EEG, neuroimaging, and behavioral experiments.

Ongoing projects include studying the effects of environmental enrichment on learning and memory in animal models.

Anatomy & Physiology

Anatomy studies the structure of organisms and their parts, while physiology focuses on how those parts function and work together.

60 Sessions
Show More

Students explore human body systems through dissections, models, and physiological measurements. They learn about homeostasis, organ systems, and clinical applications.

Research includes studying cardiovascular responses to exercise and analyzing respiratory function under different conditions.

Ecology & Environmental Biology

Ecology examines the relationships between organisms and their environment, including biodiversity, ecosystems, and conservation.

50 Sessions
Show More

This branch involves field studies, biodiversity assessments, and environmental monitoring. Students learn about ecosystem dynamics and conservation strategies.

Current projects include monitoring local bird populations and studying the impact of pollution on aquatic ecosystems.
